    Where these integrations break

    Every one of these breaks in the same handful of places, and none of them show up in a demo. The first is the inbound match. Ziwo hands you a number in one format, HubSpot stores it in another, and a naive exact-string lookup misses about half the time. I normalize both sides to E.164, then match on the last nine digits, so a missing country code or a stray space never sends a known customer to a blank screen.

    Dispositions are the next trap. Ziwo sends a short label like interested or callback. HubSpot wants a disposition GUID, and the moment someone renames a disposition in the portal, a hard-coded value starts silently dropping calls out of reporting. I keep the mapping in one table and fail safe to Connected rather than writing nothing, so a missed map costs you a label, not the whole record.

    Recordings expire. Ziwo's recording links are signed and short-lived, so if you store the URL you got at call time, it returns a 404 the following week and the playback button on the timeline does nothing. I either re-host the file or store the call id and resolve a fresh link on demand, which means the recording still plays when someone needs it during a dispute three months later.

    Timezones cause the bugs nobody can reproduce. Call timestamps arrive with a +04:00 offset, HubSpot wants epoch milliseconds, and if a script strips the offset instead of converting it, calls log a day off and managers quietly stop trusting the dashboard. I convert to UTC explicitly and keep the original timestamp alongside it.

    Then there is the quiet one: duplicate contacts. An inbound miss from an unknown number can spawn a junk contact on every ring, and within a month the database is full of single-call ghosts. I only create on answered calls past a few seconds and route the rest to a review list a human can clear in one pass.

    The last one bites when the team grows. A Ziwo extension is not a HubSpot owner id, and when a new agent joins without a mapping, their calls log with no owner and vanish from every rep dashboard. I keep that map somewhere editable and alert on the first unmapped extension, so the failure is a notification you see on day one, not a month of missing pipeline you find in a QBR.
